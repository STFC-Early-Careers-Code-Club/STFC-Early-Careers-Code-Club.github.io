```markdown
Graphs are one of the most widely used abstractions in computer science. By representing systems as nodes connected by edges, they provide a powerful way to model relationships between objects and reason about complex problems in a structured way.

In this talk we’ll take a tour through the fundamentals of graph algorithms. We’ll look at how graphs can be represented in software, explore core algorithms such as breadth-first search, depth-first search, and shortest path methods, and discuss classic optimisation problems including the Travelling Salesman Problem.

We’ll also touch on an important theme in theoretical computer science: why some problems can be solved efficiently while others quickly become computationally difficult. The aim is to provide an accessible introduction to graph algorithms and the kinds of problems they help us solve.
```
